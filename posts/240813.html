Understanding Myself & MBTI

AUG 13 2024

My interest in MBTI has been mostly on and off in the past three years. After all, it's not like sports, where there are competitions every year to keep up with. But I still find it super fascinating and I enjoy learning about it.

My first introduction to MBTI was from a TwoSetViolin video, titled “How the 16 MBTI Personality Types Make Music.” And obviously, I didn't know what any of the letters meant. Like ENTJ… what is that supposed to mean? Are they just four random letters of the alphabet?

Now I don't remember what I did after that. I mean, I was in 5th grade, and this was when I learned how to “secretly” watch YouTube in bed with the lights off until 1 am. So my lack of sleep is probably one reason why I have like no memory of this time. But I probably just searched up MBTI that same day and then decided to take the test from 16Personalities. And my result was ENTP-T.

So then I learned about the dichotomies. Extraversion vs introversion, sensing vs intuition, thinking vs feeling, judging vs perceiving, turbulent vs assertive. So I thought about my result. Hmmm, I'm probably an extravert because I like talking to people most of the time. And I use logic all the time, so T makes sense for me. And I don't plan things a lot, so I'm definitely perceiving (P). And turbulent makes sense as well, like I have a constant need to improve, I'm a perfectionist, and I have impostor syndrome to a certain extent.

But sensing vs intuition? I was like 11 years old, and I didn't understand what those words meant. So I just trusted the test results. To be fair, there are still some words on the 16Personalities test that I have to look up every time. I think my brain is just too small for those words.

My second phase of interest in MBTI was a year later when I was 12. I'm pretty sure I was on vacation in Hawaii when I decided to take the 16Personalities test again. My result was… ISTP. So I was like what? How did two letters change? And I don't fit the stereotype of being a mechanic or craftsman. Is this right?

I asked my friend group at that time if they thought I was more like an ENTP or an ISTP. But I don't think anyone really wants to read two whole articles just to tell their really cringe friend what personality type they were. Then they all shared their MBTI types, which I found fascinating. I think I still have the post-it where I wrote them down, even if I don't hang out with all of these people anymore.

I also discovered Frank James on YouTube. I really liked watching his comedy sketches, and I always tried to see if I related to the ENTP or ISTP parts. Then, I learned about the cognitive functions (btw I still think his video explains it pretty well, if you want to watch it). It was like an entire new realization for me. Like I thought it was so cool that MBTI was essentially two different systems. The simpler, surface level one being dichotomies, and the more big-brain-people one being the cognitive functions. But why did it take me a whole year to discover what it was?

I think once I learned about the cognitive functions, I started calling myself an MBTI nerd. You can see it in the identity web project I did at the start of the year in language arts. I was desperately trying to come up with 20 things, so one of them was “ISTP” and another was “interested in MBTI.”

However, it added another level of confusion for me when determining my own personality type. Especially when you have to factor in loops, grips, and certain functions being not as developed. And I recently read that sometimes, people's dominant functions are so natural to them that they don't even realize they are using it. So I could be an Fi dom without even knowing! That's kind of weird, since I've been so convinced that I'm a thinker.

I still tell people I'm an ISTP. Because to this day, I still don't have a good answer. And I suppose I just have to accept that for now. I shouldn't take it too seriously, since my personality is probably going to change as I go through high school and stuff. It's sort of hard to get an accurate result as a teenager.

But there's still hope. Maybe I can convince one of my friends to learn the cognitive functions, and then get them to type me. Then maybe, I can finally come to a conclusion for this question that has been in the back of my mind for the past 3 years.
