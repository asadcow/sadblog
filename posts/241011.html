Annoying Things - school ver.

OCT 11 2024

I am a person who gets really annoyed at trivial things. For example, I probably examined every single word in this post to make sure it was the best way to explain my thoughts. Two years ago, I made an entire Google Doc of all the things I don't like. Every so often, I go back, add new things, and change some wording. You see, that's the “examining every single word” thing.

But I was wondering, why do I seem to be the only one who is bothered by such things? Like obviously if someone is doing these things, they either don't know or don't find it strange. Why have I developed the ability to notice every detail?

So I went through that three-page list again and sorted everything into categories. I mean, there was no way anybody would read the whole three pages. And we will start with the stationery/school section, since it is the one where people commit the most faults.

  • when people fold a stapled packet not close to the staple — doesn't it cut off some of the writing?
  • stapling papers too far from the edge or not stapling it 45 degrees diagonally — if you staple it horizontally or vertically, how will you fold it correctly?

    • And I didn't know it could get worse than horizontal or vertical. One time I volunteered to help my teacher staple some papers that were just printed, and the other girl was stapling them in the opposite diagonal. I felt bad for anyone who got those packets

  • when a packet is stapled but the edges aren't even lined up
  • detaching the first page of a stapled packet by ripping it off except you did it wrong and the corner of the paper is left behind

I think you can see that staples need to be very specific

  • papers in binders getting ripped where the hole punches were
  • bent corners in a book — I used to fold the corners of books when I didn't have a bookmark. But now that I think about it, why did I do that?
  • using the eraser on a mechanical pencil until it is flat and you can no longer take the eraser out to refill the pencil lead
  • when there is half an inch of lead left but the mechanical pencil refuses to let you use it
  • when people don't push in the lead after using their mechanical pencil so the edge of their pencil pouch is really dirty
  • when people use the wrong side of binder paper — it's so dumb and if you ever need to put that thing in a binder, it will be the wrong way.
  • when people don't leave a margin on the right side of lined paper
  • when my handwriting changes throughout the notebook — it's inevitable but still annoying
  • when people hole punch papers but the middle hole punch isn't in the middle — if you put the papers in a binder, the top/bottom edges of the papers will not be lined up and that's so annoying to look at
  • when dividers or folders stick out of binders
  • when glue sticks have dried glue on them — it becomes disgusting and affects the cap closing. And you shouldn't be twisting so much of the glue out to the point where it goes over the edge
