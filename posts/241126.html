My Thoughts on Wild Life (1)

NOV 26 2024

Here's the promised continuation to my post, “NEW LIFE SERIES.” It might have sounded like I was talking about a TV series called “New Life.” I don't know if that's a real series. But it's not what I'm talking about.

Here, I wrote my comments for episodes 3, 4, and 5 (forgot to do it for Episode 2). Episode 6 was released recently, but that will have to be in Part 2 with the future episodes since this post is already long.

★ Episode 3: Snails

In my last post about this series, I said:

i also think it's interesting that they had 6 lives. i wonder if some of the wildcards in later episodes will make them more prone to taking damage? because why else would they need 6?

But after the chaos that was this session, this series could be as short as Double Life. It's impressive how Gem, Joel, Scott, Cleo, and Lizzie did not die to their snails, yet Jimmy, Scar, and Skizz managed to die so many times. Also, the skins of each snail and the sounds were so well designed. So was the name of Scar's snail (eSCARgo). I didn't understand it at first, but upon searching up, I realized it's escargot (snails as food; didn't know that before), and now I think it's by far the smartest name.

★ Episode 4: Speeding Up

This was a very needed change after the amount of deaths in the previous session. Some of the snail deaths were entertaining, but I think traps or double/triple kills are better. I was confused when everything froze in the beginning. It would've been even better if Skizz jumped down a little earlier, since him stopping 10 blocks above ground would've been so funny.

I was sad when Joel died to Jimmy, especially since creeper web kills don't really allow the person to defend themselves. But I realize that the creepers and cobwebs would've taken time to gather, so fair play. Plus, maybe it's just that I'm a Joel supporter, and that's why I was sad.

But Mumbo's lava bucket death, that was funny. He always finds the dumbest ways to die. The way he jumped off the car, thinking Grian could save him, but he took several hearts of fall damage and probably could've survived if he didn't.

★ Episode 5: Trivia Bot

It was slightly stressful when the lifers struggled with easy questions. Some of them I didn't even think could be answered wrong. And Mumbo apparently thinks he was in Third Life and Second Life. Very entertaining non-existent season, Second Life.

I got around 70 of the questions right when I did the form. Which I think is decent considering these are the POVs I watched. TL (3L?): none recently. Last Life: Jimmy a while ago, Joel. DL: Jimmy, Tango, Joel, Grian? a while ago. Limited Life: all three bad boys, Impulse first 3 episodes. SL: Joel.

I can see why I'm a top 0.4% Joel viewer.

Regarding the rewards for answering questions correctly, I thought they were just a little bit OP. Several tridents and maces, lots of wind charges and ender pearls, powered rails for TNT minecarts, etc. But the fact that the maces only had one durability (I think) sort of balanced it out, and Mumbo's mace kill was amazing. If only he didn't die like 3 times this episode…

I'm so sad about Mumbo being the first out, especially since his last two deaths were so stupid. Why did he literally walk off the tower? And why did he stand in front of the minecart? I can't tell which is dumber: letting a minecart crash into you or getting stuck on your own fence posts while escaping a warden. If I had a nickel for every time Mumbo's final death in a life series was directly or indirectly caused by an object he placed, I'd have two nickels. Which isn't a lot but it's enough to annoy Jimmy SolidarityGaming who hates the nickel meme format. Sorry Jimmy.

Anyway that's all.
